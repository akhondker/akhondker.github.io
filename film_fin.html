Film Financing Primer on how financing flows through the indie film industry

Film producers may not have been aware of the fact that they have been subject to Depression Era securities laws that essentially prevented them from promoting their projects to the investment world at large. For the past 80 years, those looking to raise funds for their film projects or film startups could not declare that they were doing so openly to the public. No matter how large their professional and social networks happen to be, the law required them to talk to investors individually, one painstaking meeting at a time. Now filmmakers will be able to advertise their fundraising publicly, so long as they make reasonable efforts that investors financing the film are accredited. With the passage of the law, promoting a film for financing publicly on blogs, tweeting, Facebook postings, and even billboards will now be legal.
